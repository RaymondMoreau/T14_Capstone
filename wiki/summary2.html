Challenges Encountered during semester

Assets and Sprite Creation

Issue with AI generated assets not being continuous and lacking consistency between generations (eg. Knight will have a sword and shield in 1 part of the animation, and just a sword in the next). To resolve this, we started sourcing our assets from free online asset websites for games requiring animated sequences.

Compatibility Issues

Something we should have given more thought too was considering any possible compatibility issues we might have had, and made a mitigation plan in case they came up. For example, when we first started hosting our games on the web we faced some issues with the resolution of the games, some of our assets would be cut off from view. Another compatibility issue we had is that Github could not support the size of each Unity game, so we had to resort to creating individual repositories for our games. Overall, our compatibility issues were mitigated but it would have been a lot more helpful to have considered these issues before they came up.

Communication and Collaboration

We had weekly meetings with our client discussing the progress of the work and any updates we had. We ended not having that many meetings as a team, we feel that we should have had more scheduled meetings and discuss internally the progress of our project. We would've liked to communicate more effectively, frequently keeping the team updated on each other's progress through our Discord chat. Given the nature of our project, it can be easy to isolate yourself while working on your own games. In the future, we would like to improve on this and make more time for ourselves as a team to communicate more effectively.

Updates to original proposal

Wasn't too many extra things tacked on, just different game ideas and features. The client was also more specific with the types of games she wanted. For example, she specified she liked having random events in her classes, and wanted us to create a randomized spinner wheel that would pick between her random events for the class. Requested that we model off of some of her google sheets games that she made to fill the void with classcraft going down (We made Quadradle as a response for this). She wanted her excel sheets of questions to be uploaded to the database which was feasible and some are currently in the database now. She also requested that we go in-person to playtest the games with her students! As an excellent form of QC/QA, we organized to go December 11th.

Deployment

Being able to host the games on GitHub pages was great for us, as most free hosting services and spotty or non-functional. At times, with Unity export to web there was issues with the resolutions when hosting on a website. Whether one had their browser zoomed or would fullscreen the game it could cause issues but for now most if not all of these are patched and our games are successfully deployed to github.
The database is hosted on a free hosting platform, meaning we can only host it for about 30 seconds before it goes down. This is unfortunate as the games won't work unless the database is up, requiring the host to sit at their computer monitoring and restart the database constantly. To combat this, we only developed two games to rely on the database while the rest used static values. This ensures that whoever accesses the site will be able to play at least some games with the database down. This is also better from a development standpoint as we wouldn't require the host to be online to test if our games work, and could continue developing independently.
